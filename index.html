Understanding SIP Returns & Taxation

How SIP Returns Are Calculated

A Systematic Investment Plan (SIP) lets you invest a fixed amount every month in a mutual fund. Each monthly installment buys units at the prevailing NAV (Net Asset Value).

Returns are calculated using compound interest. Each installment grows independently from the date it was invested until the date you redeem. Earlier installments have more time to compound, so they contribute more to the final corpus.

This is also why SIPs benefit from rupee cost averaging — you buy more units when markets are low and fewer when markets are high, smoothing out volatility over time.

The calculator uses this formula for each installment:

Future Value = Monthly Amount × (1 + monthly rate)months held

The total corpus is the sum of the future values of all individual installments.

How Tax Affects Mutual Fund Returns

In India, mutual fund gains are taxed when you redeem (sell) your units. The tax rate depends on the type of fund and the holding period of each installment.

Equity Mutual Funds:

  • STCG (Short Term): If you hold units for less than 12 months, gains are taxed at 20%.
  • LTCG (Long Term): If you hold for 12 months or more, gains are taxed at 12.5%. The first ₹1.25 lakh of LTCG per financial year is exempt.

Debt Mutual Funds (purchased after April 2023):

  • All gains are added to your income and taxed at your income tax slab rate. The earlier indexation benefit is no longer available for new purchases.

A 4% Health & Education Cess is applied on top of the income tax amount.

Why Post-Tax Returns Matter

Most SIP calculators show only pre-tax returns, which can paint an overly optimistic picture. The money you actually receive is after taxes are deducted.

For example, if your SIP grows to ₹50 lakh but you owe ₹2.5 lakh in taxes, your real take-home is ₹47.5 lakh. Planning around the pre-tax number could leave you short of your goal.

Understanding post-tax returns helps you:

  • Set more accurate financial goals.
  • Compare different investment options fairly.
  • Plan redemptions more tax-efficiently.
  • Appreciate the impact of holding period on taxation.
